Tonkotsu Ramen

Description

Tonkotsu ramen is an excellent introduction to Japanese noodle soups. Deeply flavored tonkotsu pork broth. ramen noodles and chashu pork belly come togother to create comfort in a bowl

Ingredients

  1. 8 cups tonkotsu pork broth
  2. 12 oz good quality dried ramen noodles
  3. 4 large or extra large eggs
  4. 2-3 oz enoki or other mushrooms
  5. hinly sliced green onions
  6. 2 lb pork belly
  7. 1/4 cup soy sauce
  8. 1/2 cup sake
  9. 1/2 cup mirin
  10. 1/4 cup sugar
  11. 2-3 cloves garlic – left whole
  12. 2 green onions – coarsely chopped
  13. 2 slices bacon – use good quality bacon here
  14. 1/4 cup soy sauce
  15. 2 Tbsp mirin
  16. 2 Tbsp sake
  17. 4 Tbsp shiro miso
  18. 1/2 cup shiro miso
  19. 1/4 cup sake
  20. 1/4 cup mirin
  21. pinch shichimi togarashi (optional)
  22. 1-2 tsp kosher salt – depending on how salty you like your ramen

    23. Instructions

    Chashu Pork Belly
    1. Marinate the Pork Belly:
      1. Combine soy, sake, mirin, sugar, garlic, and green onions in a Ziploc bag large enough to hold the pork belly.
      2. Stick a straw in the bag and seal the Ziploc bag up against it (so the straw is the only opening).
      3. Suck as much air out as you can and seal the bag.
    2. Sous Vide Cooking:
      1. Sous vide the pork for 10-11 hours at 170°F.
    3. Remove and Cool:
      1. Remove pork from the Ziploc bag. Discard the bag and marinade.
      2. Let the chashu pork belly cool completely.
    4. Slice the Pork:
      1. Slice across the chashu pork (so you get bacon-like slices) into 8-12 slices about 1/8 to 3/16 inches thick.
      2. Reserve the slices. Note: You won’t need all the pork for 4 servings.
    Soy Bacon Tare
    1. Prepare the Tare:
      1. Combine all ingredients in a small saucepan.
      2. Simmer at the lowest setting for about an hour. Top up with a bit of chicken stock if needed.
      3. Remove the bacon.
    Miso Tare
    1. Prepare the Tare:
      1. Combine all ingredients in a small saucepan.
      2. Simmer at the lowest setting for about 5 minutes.
    Medium Boiled Eggs
    1. Boil the Eggs:
      1. Bring enough water to cover the eggs to a boil. Prick the eggshell if you can.
      2. Boil large eggs for 6 minutes 30 seconds. If using extra-large eggs, boil them for 7 minutes 30 seconds. Adjust times slightly based on egg size.
    2. Chill and Peel:
      1. Submerge the eggs in cold or ice water to chill and stop the yolks from continuing to set.
      2. Peel the eggs. Cut in half right before serving.
    Assemble the Tonkotsu Ramen
    1. Cook the Noodles and Mushrooms:
      1. Boil the ramen noodles in plenty of water as directed by the packaging (usually 4 minutes if there’s no translation). No need to salt the water.
      2. Cook the mushrooms alongside the noodles just until softened.
    2. Fry the Chashu Pork:
      1. Gently fry the chashu pork in a non-stick skillet until lightly browned.
    3. Prepare the Bowls:
      1. Place 1/4 of whichever tare you are using in the bottom of four bowls.
      2. Ladle about 1/2 cup of tonkotsu broth into each bowl and stir to mix.
    4. Assemble the Ramen:
      1. Add the noodles to the bowls.
      2. Pour in another 1 1/2 cups of tonkotsu broth per bowl.
      3. Top with the egg, mushrooms, pork, and green onions.
